Dear Fellow Exeter Residents:

On March 10th, please consider voting for Daryl Browne to fill one of the two open seats on the Town of Exeter's Select Board.

Since 1979, New Hampshire has been my home. I was raised in Salem, then Derry, graduating from Pinkerton Academy in 1989. After college, I followed several technology jobs around the United States - from Georgia to California - but came back to our beloved state in 2001. I taught computer science at Plymouth State University for 10 years and served on the Select Board there from 2008 to 2011. While in that position, I contributed local focus and clear-headed oversight on issues that ranged from union negotiations to public safety complex construction.

My wife and I moved to Exeter in 2015, and our daughter was born here at Exeter Hospital in 2016. We have owned and operated a downtown business on Water Street since 2017, and I currently work as a programmer for a software startup based in Exeter. From running a business that helps make our historic downtown center a destination to helping push the envelope of our state's technology presence, I understand the benefits business brings as well as the responsibility it has to support our town's human and natural resources.

I believe in local government and in the importance of delegating and entrusting authority to committees that are accountable but not micromanaged. If elected, I will listen, learn and relay every town member's perspective to each issue the board oversees.

Thank you for your consideration and I look forward to serving you as a member of our Select Board for the next three years.

Sincerely,

Daryl Browne
